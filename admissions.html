Admissions Process

Welcome to Doge Gap homeschool! We are excited that you are considering our program for your child's education. Our admission process is designed to ensure a smooth transition and provide the necessary information for enrollemnt.
Below is an overview of the steps involved:

Step 1: Submit an Application

The first step is to complete and submit the application form. You will need to obtain a physical copy of the application form from our admissions office. The application form collects essential information about your child and helps us understand their educational background and needs.

Step 2: Schedule an Interview or Assessment

Upon receiving your completed application, we will schedule an interview or assessment, depending on the grade level and program. This step allows us to get to know your child better and assess their readiness for our homeschool program. It is also an opportunity for you to ask any questions you may have.

Step 3: Provide Required Documents

As part of the admissions process, we require certain documents to complete your child's enrollment. These may include:

  • Proof of age (birth certificate or passport)
  • Previous academic records or transcripts (if applicable)
  • Immunization records (as required by local regulations)
  • Any other relevant educational or medical documentation

Please have these documents prepared and ready to submit upon request.

Step 4: Acceptance and Enrollment

Once the application, interview or assessment, and required documents are reviewed, we will notify you of the admission decision. If accepted, you will receive an acceptance letter along with instructions for completing the enrollment process.

During the enrollment process, you will be required to submit additional forms, such as emergency contact information, health forms, and media release permissions. These forms ensure that we have the necessary information to provide a safe and enriching learning environment for your child.

Upon completing the enrollment forms and submitting any required fees, your child's place in our homeschool program will be secured.

Step 5: Orientation

Prior to the start of the academic year, we conduct an orientation session for enrolled students and their parents. This session provides important information about our curriculum, policies, and expectations, allowing for a smooth transition into our homeschool program.

Admissions Policies and Standards

Equal Opportunity Admissions

Doge Gap Homeschool is committed to providing equal opportunity in its admissions process. We do not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, gender, national origin, disability, or any other protected status.

Age Requirements

Applicants must meet the age requirements set forth by the applicable educational regulations. These age requirements ensure that students are developmentally ready for the curriculum and learning experiences offered at Doge Gap Homeschool.

Enrollment Capacity

Our enrollment capacity is limited to ensure that each student receives the attention and support they need. Admissions are granted on a first-come, first-served basis, subject to availability. We encourage interested parents to apply early to secure a spot for their child.

Code of Conduct

At Doge Gap Homeschool, we maintain a positive and inclusive learning environment that promotes respect, kindness, and responsibility. All students are expected to adhere to our code of conduct, which includes:

  • Respecting the rights and dignity of others
  • Behaving in a manner that supports a safe and nurturing learning environment
  • Engaging in honest and ethical conduct
  • Showing respect for diversity and cultural differences
  • Adhering to school policies and rules

Any violations of the code of conduct may result in disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal from Doge Gap Homeschool.

Withdrawal and Dismissal

In the event that a student needs to withdraw from Doge Gap Homeschool or if there are concerns about a student's behavior or academic progress, the school administration will work closely with the student and their family to address the situation. If necessary, the school reserves the right to dismiss a student based on a review of the circumstances and in accordance with our policies.

For detailed information on our admissions policies, standards, and code of conduct, please refer to our official Student Handbook or contact our admissions office.

Our admissions team is available to guide you through each step of the process and answer any questions you may have. We look forward to partnering with you in your child's educational journey!
